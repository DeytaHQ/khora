# Perseverance: Science Instruments

Perseverance carries seven primary science instruments.

## Mast instruments

- **Mastcam-Z** — a pair of zoomable cameras on the mast for stereo and panoramic color imaging.
- **SuperCam** — fires a laser to vaporize rock at a distance and analyzes the glowing plasma for chemistry and mineralogy; it also records sound.

## Robotic-arm turret

The instruments mounted on the turret at the end of the robotic arm are:

- **PIXL** — an X-ray fluorescence spectrometer that maps the elemental composition of rocks at fine scale.
- **SHERLOC** — an ultraviolet Raman spectrometer and imager that searches for organics and minerals; it carries the WATSON close-up camera.
- the **coring drill**, which cuts rock cores into sample tubes.

## Body and other instruments

- **MEDA** — the Mars Environmental Dynamics Analyzer, a weather station measuring wind, temperature, humidity, pressure, and dust.
- **RIMFAX** — a ground-penetrating radar that images rock layers beneath the rover.
- **MOXIE** — see results below.

## MOXIE oxygen results

MOXIE, the Mars Oxygen In-Situ Resource Utilization Experiment, pulled oxygen from Mars' carbon-dioxide atmosphere by solid-oxide electrolysis. Across 16 runs it produced 122 grams of oxygen in total, peaking at 12 grams per hour — twice NASA's goal — at 98 percent purity or better. It demonstrated a way for future crews to make breathable oxygen and rocket propellant on Mars.
