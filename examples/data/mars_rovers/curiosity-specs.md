# Curiosity: Vehicle Specifications

## Size and mass

Curiosity is about the size of a small SUV — roughly 3 meters long (10 feet), 2.7 meters wide (9 feet), and 2.2 meters tall (7 feet) at the top of its mast, not counting the arm. It weighs about 899 kilograms (1,982 pounds) and uses a 2.1-meter (7-foot) robotic arm to place tools on rocks. When it launched in 2011 it was the largest and most capable rover ever sent to Mars.

## Cameras and instruments

Curiosity carries 17 cameras and a kit of 10 science instruments, including a laser that vaporizes pinpoint spots of rock and a drill that collects powdered samples. Seven of its camera "eyes" are mounted on the mast.

## Wheels and power

Curiosity rolls on six aluminum wheels, each 50 centimeters (20 inches) in diameter with 24 treads, and can climb over obstacles nearly 65 centimeters (about 25 inches) tall. A Multi-Mission Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generator (MMRTG) lets it operate continuously for at least one Martian year (687 Earth days).
