# Perseverance: Mission Overview

NASA's Mars 2020 Perseverance rover landed in Jezero Crater on February 18, 2021. It searches for signs of ancient microbial life, collects rock and regolith samples for potential return to Earth, and helps prepare for human exploration of Mars.

## Science goals

Perseverance pursues four science objectives:

- **Habitability** — identify past environments that could have supported microbial life.
- **Biosignatures** — seek signs of possible ancient microbial life in those environments.
- **Sample caching** — collect and cache core samples of Martian rock and regolith. Perseverance is the first mission to gather and store Martian samples for a future return campaign.
- **Prepare for humans** — test technologies for future human expeditions, including making oxygen from the Martian atmosphere.

## Ingenuity helicopter

Perseverance carried the Ingenuity helicopter to Mars beneath its belly. On April 19, 2021, Ingenuity made the first powered, controlled flight on another planet. Designed for up to five test flights, it ultimately completed 72 flights over nearly three years — about 129 minutes aloft and 17 kilometers covered — before its final flight on January 18, 2024.
