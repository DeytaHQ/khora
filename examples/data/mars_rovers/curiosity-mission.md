# Curiosity: Mission Overview

NASA's Mars Science Laboratory rover, Curiosity, landed in Gale Crater on August 6, 2012. Its mission is to assess whether Mars ever had an environment able to support microbial life.

## Gale Crater and Mount Sharp

Curiosity landed on the plains of Gale Crater at a site named Bradbury Landing. At the crater's center stands Mount Sharp (Aeolis Mons), a layered mountain that rises about 5.5 kilometers (3.4 miles) above the crater floor. The rover has been climbing Mount Sharp since 2014, reading its rock layers like pages to track how the Martian environment changed over time.

## Key discovery

At "Yellowknife Bay," Curiosity drilled the "Sheepbed" mudstone and found sulfur, nitrogen, oxygen, phosphorus, and carbon — key ingredients for life. The results showed that ancient Mars once had the right chemistry to support living microbes, fulfilling the mission's primary goal.
